On It.

The sky gathers into a dark, brooding weight, then breaks—light slipping through torn clouds in quiet, deliberate beams, touching the cooling desert sands. The casino lights shimmer in the distance.

Out in the scrub, a low-tier mob guy leans on his shovel beside a shallow grave, catching his breath. He squints at the horizon, watching gold spill over the mountains, thinking to himself: They don’t pay me enough for this shit, but the benefits are great.

For a moment, he stands there, contemplating something larger than himself.

His phone buzzes. He checks it. A message from his boss: “Did you take care of that thing?”

He types back, "On it", sighs, and slips the phone back into his pocket.

Then he exhales, grips the shovel, and gets back to work.
